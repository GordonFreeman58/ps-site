Palestinian Dishes


Traditional Palestinian dishes are diverse, rooted in history, and divided into various categories.

We have fresh salads, desserts, hearty meat dishes, and family-style meals. I’ll highlight a few and share a source to explore more of Palestine’s flavors and culture.




SWEETS



Palestinian sweets, particularly Baklava , are a delightful part of the region's culinary heritage. This indulgent pastry consists of layers of thin phyllo dough filled with chopped nuts and sweetened with syrup or honey. Often enjoyed during special occasions, baklava symbolizes celebration and togetherness, showcasing the artistry of Palestinian desserts.

MEALS


Palestinian meals are rich and flavorful, often featuring comforting dishes like Musakhan. This traditional favorite consists of tender chicken marinated in sumac, served on a bed of warm, flatbread, and topped with caramelized onions. It's a heartwarming dish that embodies the essence of Palestinian hospitality, making it perfect for family gatherings.

SALAD



Palestinian salads are vibrant and refreshing, crafted from a variety of local vegetables and herbs, often shared at family gatherings. A prime example is Tabbouleh , a traditional dish, a colorful twist on a classic Middle Eastern dish. This herby, lemon-infused salad pairs perfectly with fish and stands out as a delightful light lunch or snack .
